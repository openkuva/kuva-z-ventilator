Disassembling the machine for cleaning

Routine cleaning restores and disinfects both the exterior and interior of the machine. Some disassembly is required to do this.

Doing these steps exposes the cleaner to viruses, bacteria, and other substances that may be harmful, and so should be done wearing appropriate PPE.

There is also a risk that another person may not realize that these components are contaminated, and that the parts may contaminate any surface they are placed on.

Cleaning the machine

With the machine disassembled as above,

  1. remove and properly dispose of the filters
  2. place TBD - all the parts that come on contact with patient expirations in disinfectant for the recommended time
  3. immediately disinfect the surface the parts were lying on
  4. after the time is up, wash the soaked parts in sterile water to remove the disinfectant then allow to dry
  5. reassemble, inserting new filters
